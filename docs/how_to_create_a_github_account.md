# How to Create a GitHub Account

GitHub is a website where code projects are stored and shared. You need a free account to access this project.

## Create Your Account

1. Go to https://github.com in your web browser
2. Click **Sign up** in the top right corner
3. Enter your **email address**
4. Create a **password** (at least 15 characters, or 8 characters with a number and lowercase letter)
5. Choose a **username** — this is your public identity on GitHub (e.g., `jsmith42`)
6. Complete the verification puzzle (you may need to identify images or solve a simple challenge)
7. Click **Create account**
8. GitHub will send a verification email — open your email and click the link to verify
9. When asked to personalise your experience, you can click **Skip this step** at the bottom

## What to Do Next

Once your account is created, send your **GitHub username** (the one you chose in step 5) to the person who is sharing this project with you. They will add you as a collaborator so you can access the code.

You will then receive an **invitation email** from GitHub. Click the link in that email to accept, and you will have access to the project.

## Tips

- **Remember your username and password** — you will need them later when downloading the project
- The free plan gives you everything you need; you do not need to pay for anything
- If you already have a GitHub account, you can skip this and just share your existing username
